## Question

Can the exact source-free skeleton (`261` ops, `208` copies, `53`
literal runs) be generated by a compact source-free grammar rather
than retained as a materialized atlas?

## Hard Boundary

- Skeleton atlas records: `261`.
- Copy-source fields still external: `208`.
- Literal payload chunks/digits still external: `53` / `266`.
- External dependency fields after skeleton: `261`.
- Total materialized records after skeleton: `522`.

The main blocker is therefore the skeleton atlas itself. Source choice
and literal payload remain real, but they are secondary to deriving the
operation sequence.

## Grammar Gate

| Diagnostic | Value |
|---|---:|
| Context families | `13` |
| Distinct op labels | `97` |
| Best context | `book_mod10_x_op_index` |
| Best exact books | `3/60` |
| Best op hits | `14/261` |
| Best net vs atlas label bits | `660.028` bits |
| Prefix/holdout cover-all-test-books cells | `0/5` |

The tested source-free grammar families are rejected. Even granting
book lengths, the best full-fit context explains only `3/60` books
and `14/261` operation positions, is `+660.028` bits worse than a
label-atlas lower bound after corrections, and has `0/5` holdout
cells that generate all test books.

## Decision

- No source-free skeleton generator is promoted.
- The exact skeleton remains a materialized atlas, not a derivation.
- This falsifies simple grammars over operation index, prior operation,
  book length, remaining length, phase, and book-mod/order features.
- Copy-source selection should remain secondary until the skeleton is
  generated or a stronger target-stream account appears.
- Literal payload remains external and should be audited separately.
- Row0 remains exogenous and unchanged.
- No plaintext, translation, semantic reading, or case reopening is introduced.
