## Question

Can the `261` operation lengths be generated by simple
source-free weighted allocation once book length and literal/copy
sequence are granted?

## Result

- Books/operations: `60` / `261`.
- Models tested: `10`.
- Best model: `learned_by_shape_x_type_x_position`.
- Best model kind: `weighted`.
- Best exact books: `14/60`.
- Best row hits: `74/261`.
- Best row errors: `187`.
- Best absolute error: `2430`.
- Best paid records: `363` vs exact length fields `261`.
- Paid-record delta vs exact length fields: `+102`.
- Prefix/holdout cover-all cells: `0/5`.

The best full-corpus model is `learned_by_shape_x_type_x_position`,
but it still reproduces only `14/60` books and pays more than the
exact length-field atlas after corrections. Prefix/holdout has no
cover-all cell.

## Decision

- No operation-length generator is promoted.
- Type-weighted source-free allocation is rejected as a structural generator.
- Operation lengths remain retained unless a later joint parser derives them with copy availability/source state.
- This does not change the compression bound.
- Row0 remains exogenous and unchanged.
- No plaintext, translation, semantic reading, or case reopening is introduced.
