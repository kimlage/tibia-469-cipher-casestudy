## Question

Can the target digit stream that downstream copy-source collapse needs
be generated by a simple source-free digit process, rather than being
declared as payload?

## Result

- All-book digits tested: `11263`.
- All70 aggregate validation test digits: `24559`.
- Derived-book digits tested: `9567`.
- Derived60 aggregate validation test digits: `24559`.
- Best all70 model: `prev2_digits` at `2.068621` bits/digit, `30779.978` aggregate bits vs uniform.
- All70 beats shuffled best p95: `True`.
- Best derived60 model: `prev2_digits` at `2.108869` bits/digit, `29791.523` aggregate bits vs uniform.
- Derived60 beats shuffled best p95: `True`.
- Target digit Markov clue promoted: `True`.

The result promotes a narrow mechanical clue: a second-order digit
process (`prev2_digits`) is strongly predictive under prefix/suffix
validation and beats same-book-histogram shuffled controls in both
scopes. This is not a complete generator. The model still emits the
books only by paying residual arithmetic bits and does not explain
operation chunk endpoints or exact copied payload choices.

## Decision

- A target-digit Markov clue is promoted.
- No source-free target digit process generator is promoted.
- The target-stream blocker remains open.
- This does not change the compression bound.
- Row0 remains exogenous and unchanged.
- No plaintext, translation, semantic reading, or case reopening is introduced.
