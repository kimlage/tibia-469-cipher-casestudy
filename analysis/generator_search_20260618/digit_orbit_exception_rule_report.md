## Interpretation

The side orientation is simple: among mixed orbits, even `x` puts the
lower symbol on the `x6` cell and odd `x` puts it on `x9`. The hard
part is selecting exactly which four `x` values are mixed. The best
selector is exact only because it is effectively a compound signature
over nine cases. Controls find exact low-cost selectors and perfect
orientation rules often enough that this remains a descriptive
microfit, not the original matrix formula.
