## Question

Are the 70 book lengths mechanically generated by source-free rules, or
are they still declared as a residual ledger used by later parsers?

## Ledger

- Book count: `70`.
- Length range: `35..294`.
- Unique lengths: `51`.
- Raw gamma length bits: `1030`.
- Active signed-Rice ledger: `anchor=151`, `k=5`, `566` bits.
- Active gain vs raw gamma: `464` bits.

The active ledger is a real compression improvement over independent
gamma-coded lengths, but it still declares residuals for the exact
length sequence.

## Generation Gate

- Policies tested: `8`.
- Best full-fit policy: `book_mod10_median`.
- Best full-fit exact lengths: `14/70`.
- Best full-fit mean absolute error: `37.886`.
- Best lookup context: `book_index_lookup` with `70/70` exact lengths.
- Lookup context count: `70` length-bearing contexts.
- Prefix/holdout cover-all cells: `0/6`.
- Prefix/holdout any-exact cells: `4/6`.

The only exact full-corpus representation is a book-index lookup carrying
length payloads. Simple source-free policies do not generate the exact
lengths, and prefix/holdout never covers all future books.

## Decision

- No book-length generator is promoted.
- Book lengths remain external declared residuals, even though the residual ledger is compact.
- Gates that grant book length are still granting a real dependency.
- This does not change the compression bound.
- Row0 remains exogenous and unchanged.
- No plaintext, translation, semantic reading, or case reopening is introduced.
